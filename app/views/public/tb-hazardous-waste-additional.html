What is a transboundary hazardous waste transfer?

This refers to the shipment, export, or import of dangerous waste materials - such as toxic chemicals, infectious medical waste, or explosive substances - across international borders for disposal, recycling, or recovery. These movements are strictly regulated by international law to prevent environmental damage and protect human health.

What is hazardous waste?

Hazardous waste is any discarded material that poses a substantial or potential threat to public health or the environment due to its chemical, biological, or physical properties.

What does disposal mean?

Waste is taken to a specialised facility where it is sorted, and aggregated before being transported to final disposal sites like landfills or recycling centres.
